SWOT

Strengths

  • True 1-buyer exclusivity, enforced in code — unique in this market
  • Zero-commitment entry: free browse, ${{ '%.0f'|format(s.price_raw) }} first dollar vs $40-119/mo paywalls
  • Near-zero marginal cost: own scrapers + ~$0.001 Serper traces vs competitors' data-licensing costs
  • {{ s.owner_pct }}% owner-named records across 7 distress types incl. probate/divorce most tools skip
  • Self-healing automated pipeline (verified end-to-end in production)

Weaknesses

  • Trace-on-purchase not built yet — until it is, the ${{ '%.0f'|format(s.price_traced) }} product only exists on the {{ "{:,}".format(s.traced_n) }} pre-traced leads
  • {{ s.counties }} counties vs incumbents' "nationwide" — thin outside FL/MI/CA/TN
  • Zero brand, reviews, or SEO; unbranded infrastructure URL
  • No retention hooks (alerts, saved searches) — nothing pulls buyers back
  • Solo-operator key-person risk on scraper maintenance

Opportunities

  • Trace the backlog → turns ~{{ "{:,}".format((s.total * s.owner_pct / 100)|int) }} raw leads into ${{ '%.0f'|format(s.price_traced) }} inventory almost free
  • Exclusivity as the wedge: "the lead you buy disappears for everyone else" — no incumbent can copy without killing their model
  • Probate + divorce cross-sell (specialists charge quote-based premiums for less)
  • Price ladder: traced leads clear $30+ elsewhere; you charge ${{ '%.0f'|format(s.price_traced) }}
  • Edge Landings website services as second revenue stream on the same audience

Threats

  • Source fragility: county sites redesign/block without notice (Williamson already migrated once) — mitigated, not eliminated, by auto-detection
  • PropStream/Batch could add a pay-per-lead tier and crush on brand — speed to niche loyalty is the defense
  • Legal-notice aggregators (mipublicnotices, floridapublicnotices) could add ToS/paywalls — diversify source types per county
  • TCPA/DNC compliance on traced phones — publish a compliance note; buyers bear outreach duty
  • Foreclosure volume is cyclical — probate/divorce/tax hedge the cycle
